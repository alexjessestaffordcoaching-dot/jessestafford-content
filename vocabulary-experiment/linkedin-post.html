LinkedIn Post — The Vocabulary Experiment

Platform: LinkedIn | Target: 3-5 paragraphs, 30-second read Status: FINAL — Pending Jesse's approval

I spent weeks saying "I need to go argue with the helpdesk." Every time. It was accurate. It was also setting the tone before I walked in the door.

I changed one word. "I'm going to have a conversation with the helpdesk." Same task. Same situation. Different energy walking in. And a different response from them.

Your team is reading your word choices constantly. When you say you're going to "deal with" something, they hear friction. When you say you're going to "work through" it, they hear problem-solving. Same meaning. Different signal. The words you choose automatically are teaching your team how to read you.

The shift isn't about being more positive. It's about choosing the word that reflects what you actually intend, not what you fear might happen.

If your team's responses feel guarded or flat, listen to yourself for three days before you try to fix anything else.
