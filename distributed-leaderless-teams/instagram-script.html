[HOOK — look directly at camera]

Your team keeps escalating decisions to you that you think they should handle themselves.

That is not an initiative problem. That is a design problem.

[PAUSE — shift slightly]

Here is what I see leaders do wrong: they hand off ownership without building three things first.

The first one is scope. Not just "you are responsible for this area." That is not ownership. Ownership means you know exactly what decisions fall inside your boundary, what requires escalation, and what success looks like. Without that clarity, people default to asking.

The second one is context. Before you hand anything off, tell them why it matters. Not the process. The outcome the process is protecting. When people have context, they can adapt. When they only have process, they freeze when the situation changes.

The third one is repetition. There is an old saying that people need to hear something seven times before it sticks. It is seven times per person. If you said the goal once at the start of the quarter and moved on, it has not landed yet.

[CLOSE — lean forward slightly]

Distributed leadership is not about trusting your team more.

It is about building the conditions that make trust possible.

If you are the only person who can do something your team depends on, that is not strength. That is a gap in your design.

Build the system.
