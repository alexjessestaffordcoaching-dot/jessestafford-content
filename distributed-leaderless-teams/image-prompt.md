## Shared Elements

**Core situation:** A leader realizes their team cannot act independently because the system that would let them was never built.

**Context box text:** DISTRIBUTED LEADERSHIP IS AN ARCHITECTURE, NOT AN ATTITUDE

**Emotional tone:** Direct and focused — Jesse's expression should be calm and deliberate, the look of someone who has solved this problem before and is showing the mechanism. Not anxious, not triumphant.

## Concept 1 — The System

**Visual metaphor:** A circuit board or junction box with multiple output paths, each labeled with a different area of ownership. Jesse stands at one side looking across the board, not in the center of it. The paths fan out from the board toward the edges of the frame. Jesse is positioned at the threshold — he designed the system, but the paths run independently of him.

**First-second read:** Multiple independent paths extending outward from a central panel, with Jesse at the edge rather than the hub. Viewer reads: the work is distributed, not centralized.

**Compositional approach:** Wide shot. Junction box or circuit panel fills the center-right two-thirds of the frame. Jesse is positioned in the lower-left, smaller in scale, looking toward the panel from the edge. His posture is calm and deliberate — he built this, he is not managing it. Each output path extends beyond the frame, suggesting systems in motion.

Background: subtle steampunk mechanical texture in the upper-right, supporting the system theme without competing.

Context box:
Uppercase sans-serif text inside a solid white (#ffffff) rectangle with a sharp charcoal (#333333) border. Placed at the top of the panel. Text: DISTRIBUTED LEADERSHIP IS AN ARCHITECTURE, NOT AN ATTITUDE

Tone guardrails:

One person at the table has a document or simple reference in front of them, indicating they own something. The others are engaged with each other. Jesse is present but not directing — his posture says "I am here if you need me" not "I am running this."

**First-second read:** A group in conversation, Jesse at the edge not the head. Viewer reads: ownership is distributed across the people in the room.

The people across from him are engaged with each other. Jesse's body language is open and observant, not directive. One figure has a simple reference document — a visual cue of ownership.

## Concept 3 — The Reframe

**Unexpected angle:** Scale inversion. A large architectural blueprint pinned to a wall fills most of the frame — it is detailed, structured, annotated. Jesse stands in the lower-left, small against the blueprint, studying it. But the blueprint is not of a building. Its labeled sections read like team ownership: "Decisions," "Escalation," "Goals." The blueprint is the leadership architecture itself, made literal and legible. The irony: leaders think of architecture as buildings, not teams. The point: your team structure is as designed — or as undesigned — as any physical structure.

**First-second read:** A large detailed blueprint filling the frame, Jesse small and studying it. Viewer reads: the leadership system itself is the design object.

**Why it works:** The blueprint makes the post's central metaphor visible — distributed leadership is architecture. Seeing it rendered as a literal blueprint makes the concept land differently than a description ever could.
