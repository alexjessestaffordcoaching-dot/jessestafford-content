Six percent of young professionals say leadership is their primary career goal.

That number changes how I see every room I walk into. In most meetings, most people are hoping someone else will step up and decide. They are just relieved when someone else takes the accountability.

The person willing to decide is almost always the one the room will follow, regardless of title.

You are not competing for authority. You are filling a vacancy.

If most of the people in that room would rather someone else led, what are you still waiting for?
