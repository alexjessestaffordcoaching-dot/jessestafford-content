## Shared Elements

**Core situation:** An engineer is doing strong work that the people making career decisions cannot see — not because the work is poor, but because the engineer was never told that visibility is a skill separate from performance. The leader in the frame understands this. The engineer has not yet been told.

**Context box text:** THEY CANNOT PROMOTE WORK THEY CANNOT SEE

**Emotional tone:** Calm and grounded. Jesse has watched this pattern play out many times. His expression is steady and engaged — the look of someone who understands the situation clearly and knows what needs to be said. Not frustrated. Not lecturing. Slightly forward-leaning, as if in real conversation. No broad smile — this is a serious but human moment.

## Concept 1 — The System

**Visual metaphor:** A signal relay panel — an antenna array or transmission routing board where signals are actively being generated (the engineer's work is real and ongoing) but the routing path bends away from any receiver. The work is transmitting. Nothing is receiving it.

**First-second read:** A broadcasting system is running at full power, transmitting to a blank wall. The signal goes nowhere. A small figure stands to the side, studying the routing, understanding exactly what is wrong.

**Compositional approach:** Wide establishing shot. The relay panel and its routing diagram fill the right two-thirds of the frame. Jesse stands in the lower-left at roughly one-third the scale of the panel, turned slightly toward it, not touching it. He is observing, not operating. The signal path on the panel visibly terminates at a wall or open space — no receiver, no destination. Upper-left quadrant is open negative space.

## Concept 3 — The Reframe

**Unexpected angle:** Absent element. A theater spotlight is lit and aimed at a specific spot on a stage — but the thing the spotlight should be illuminating is sitting in shadow just outside its beam. The work exists. The light exists. They are not aligned. Jesse stands near the light source at the frame's edge, looking at the gap between where the light falls and where the work actually is.

**First-second read:** A spotlight illuminates an empty space. Just outside its reach, something important sits in darkness. The viewer understands immediately: the problem is not the light, not the work — it is the alignment.

**Why it works:** The irony is exact and immediate. Nothing is broken. The spotlight works. The work is real. They simply are not pointed at each other. Jesse's position near the light source — not near the dark shape — communicates that the fix starts with directing the light, not doing more work. This inverts the reader's assumption that the engineer needs to do more to get noticed.

**Emotional tone note for Concept 3:** Jesse's expression shifts here — slightly more analytical and curious than the other two concepts. He is studying the geometry of the problem, not just observing it. Still calm. A slight tilt of the head, eyes tracking between the spotlight and the dark shape.
