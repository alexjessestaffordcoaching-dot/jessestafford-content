The best performers I have watched try to get promoted are often the last ones to move.

Not because they lacked skill. Because they had made themselves too difficult to lose. Their knowledge lived in their heads. Their relationships lived in their calendars. Their organizations needed them exactly where they were.

Being indispensable in your current role is not a path to the next one. It is the reason you stay.

The question promotion decisions actually come down to is not "Is this person ready?" It is "Can we afford to move them?" Those are completely different questions, and only one of them has anything to do with your performance review.

If your manager has ever told you they cannot afford to lose you where you are, what does that tell you about what you need to build before that changes?
