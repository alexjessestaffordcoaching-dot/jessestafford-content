**SCRIPT:**

If your manager has ever told you they cannot afford to lose you in your current role — that is not a compliment.

That is the reason you are not getting promoted.

Here is what nobody explains: promotion decisions are not performance decisions. They are organizational decisions. The question is not whether you are ready. The question is whether the organization can afford to move you.

And if the only person who knows how your systems work is you, if the only one who holds your team's key relationships is you — they cannot.

The path out is not working harder. It is making yourself replaceable on purpose. Teaching what you know while you are doing it. Putting your people in rooms where they build relationships without you. Documenting the things that live only in your head.

The leaders who advance fastest are not the ones with the most knowledge. They are the ones whose absence would create the least disruption.

So what happens to your team's work if you are gone for two weeks?
