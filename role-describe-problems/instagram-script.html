When I got promoted to director, nobody told me the job had changed.

I kept solving every problem that came my way. I thought that was the job. That is what I was good at. That is what got me promoted.

What I did not realize is that at director level, solving is not the job anymore. Describing is.

Your value is not in having the answer. It is in naming the problem clearly enough that the right person can solve it without you.

When you keep solving, your team stops thinking. Every problem flows to you. You become the bottleneck. And you wonder why nobody seems to operate at the right level.

The shift is small. The next time someone brings you a problem, ask them one question before you say anything.

"Walk me through what you see as the problem."

That is it. Let them describe it. Add what they missed. Then ask who should own it.

That is director-level work.

When did you realize the job had changed — and what did you do differently?
