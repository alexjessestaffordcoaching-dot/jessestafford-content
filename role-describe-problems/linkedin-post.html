When I got promoted to director, my instinct was to keep doing what had always worked.

I kept solving. I kept handing back answers. I kept being the fastest person in the room with a solution. Within a year, my original team had fallen into obscurity. Not because they were not capable. Because I had never once described the problem to them. I had just solved it.

The job is not to solve problems anymore. It is to describe them clearly enough that the right person can. That shift sounds simple. Most directors miss it entirely.

Solving is how you got promoted. Describing is how you operate at this level. The two are not the same job.

If you are still solving every problem that reaches your desk, what does that tell you about the judgment you are building in the people around you?
