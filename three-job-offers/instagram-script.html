INSTAGRAM VIDEO SCRIPT (~65 seconds)

---

You have been telling your manager about your goals in every one-on-one.

You want to move into leadership. You say it clearly. You have been saying it for months.

[pause]

And you assume that means the right people know.

They do not.

Here is what nobody tells you. Your manager is not the only person deciding your promotion. Their boss has a say. Their peers have a say. And most of the time, those people have no idea what you want, because your manager never told them.

I learned this the hard way. Two offers came in from outside my company. When I sat down with my manager's boss to talk through the decision, he told me he had no idea I wanted to lead.

Months of conversations with my manager. Zero of them reached the person above him.

Your manager knows. The question is whether the people who actually make the decision know.

So this week, ask yourself: who above your manager knows exactly what you want from your career?
