SCRIPT:

Your team grew by 50% or more. Your 1-on-1s are monthly now.

I want to be honest with you: that is not a scheduling problem. It is a leadership philosophy problem.

Here is what actually happens when your span grows. The meeting stays on the calendar. What disappears is the career conversation, the feedback your team member would never raise in a group, the early problem you catch before it becomes a real one.

The managers who come through this with their teams intact are not the ones who found a better agenda template. They are the ones who decided early what the 1-on-1 was actually for. And then they cut other things to protect it.

So before you touch your calendar, ask yourself: what do I believe the 1-on-1 is for?

That answer determines everything else.
