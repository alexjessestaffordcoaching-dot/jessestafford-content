Your headcount grew by 50% or more. Your calendar filled. Your 1-on-1s became monthly.

That's not a capacity problem. That's a philosophy problem.

When span grows, the meeting stays on the calendar. What disappears first is the real conversation. The career discussion, the early conflict you catch before it spreads, the feedback that only surfaces when someone believes you actually have time for them. The template gets tighter. Quarterly starts to feel like enough. Nothing dramatic happens. The 1-on-1s just quietly stop being the thing they were.

The managers who come through significant span growth with their people intact are not the ones who found a better scheduling system. They're the ones who decided early what the 1-on-1 was actually for. They cut other things to protect it, not the other way around.

When your span grew significantly, what was the first thing you stopped asking your people about?
