Your promotion decision does not happen when you ask for it.

It happens months earlier, in conversations you weren't a part of. By the time you walk in to ask, the people coming out as clear candidates were already known to multiple people in the room.

Most leaders start building their case after that window has already closed.

If the people making that call cannot name you or describe what you have delivered, your manager is asking them to take something on faith. That is a much harder ask than it sounds.

Who in your organization, beyond your direct manager, could name one thing you have delivered in the last six months?
