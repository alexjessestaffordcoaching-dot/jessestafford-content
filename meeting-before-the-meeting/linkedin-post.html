Most people prepare for their performance review.

The leaders who get promoted prepared for the conversations that happened months before it. The ones they were never invited to.

Promotions get decided before the review. Your manager talks to their peers. Your work gets described by people who have heard of it, or have not. The case you built on paper lands in a room full of people who either already know what you bring to the table, or are hearing your name for the first time.

This is not about lobbying. It is about understanding how decisions about your career actually get made.

The leaders who advance fastest are doing something the others skip. They are building relationships before they need them. Peers, cross-functional stakeholders, the people their manager listens to. When the promotion conversation happens, those people already have something to say.

You cannot build those relationships on demand. By the time you need them, it is too late to start.

The good news: the work is not complicated. It does not require a strategy. It requires showing up consistently in the conversations where your peers are already building theirs.

What relationship are you building right now that has nothing to do with your current project?
