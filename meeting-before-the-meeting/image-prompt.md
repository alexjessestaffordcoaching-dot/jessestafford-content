## Shared Elements

**Core situation:** A leader prepares a strong case and walks into a meeting where every person in the room has already made up their mind — through conversations that happened without them.

**Context box text:** THE DECISION WAS MADE BEFORE THE MEETING

**Emotional tone:** Direct and focused. Jesse's expression is calm and clear-eyed — the look of someone who has figured out something that most people haven't. Not smug. Not surprised. Observant and deliberate.

---

## Concept 1 — The System

**Visual metaphor:** A formal boardroom viewed through an open doorway or glass partition. Each chair has a small folded card or paper already placed on it — the decisions pre-laid before anyone has arrived. The room is empty. Jesse stands at the threshold, small in the frame, looking in from outside.

**First-second read:** A man at the door of a room where the outcome is already set. He is outside it. The papers on the chairs tell you the vote is done. The meeting hasn't started and it's already over.

**Compositional approach:** Jesse small and peripheral in the lower-left, at the threshold. The meeting room fills the center-right two-thirds of the frame. The spatial positioning communicates: outside something that was decided without him.

### Full Expanded Prompt

COMPOSITION — most important:
Wide establishing shot, 16:9. A long formal conference table with five chairs runs from center to right, viewed through an open doorway or glass wall partition. On each chair: a small folded card or paper — pre-placed votes or decision slips, visible and distinct. The room is completely empty of people. Jesse stands in the lower-left quadrant of the frame at the threshold of the doorway — not inside, not walking away. He faces into the room, slightly tilted forward, looking at the pre-placed cards. His scale is notably smaller than the room and table — perhaps one-quarter of the frame height. Upper-left holds open white space. The mechanical background elements appear as faint schematic linework in the walls and ceiling of the corridor, suggesting pipework or systems running behind the architecture. Nothing competes with the central image of the empty room with its predetermined seats.

Art style spec: Crisp, uniform charcoal ink outlines (#333333), medium line weight throughout. Comic book precision — not sterile flat-art, not heavy superhero cross-hatching. Lines should suggest a technical patent drawing or a well-crafted indie comic panel. Loose, organic watercolor fills — visibly hand-painted. Color intentionally bleeds slightly outside the ink lines or leaves small white gaps at boundaries. Wet-edge textures and transparency variations are required — these are features, not errors. No digital gradients. No glow effects. No perfectly aligned fills. Background: clean white paper texture (#ffffff) with steampunk mechanical elements — objects have exposed mechanics — rendered in charcoal linework as abstract environmental texture. Mechanical background elements suggest systems and structure without being literal or dominant. 16:9 aspect ratio, single panel.

Color palette: Primary watercolor wash: #100299 (deep blue). Canvas and negative space: #ffffff. Linework and shadows: #333333. Shadows are a semi-transparent charcoal watercolor wash — never solid black fill. The meeting table and chairs carry a cool charcoal wash. The folded cards on each chair have a subtle warm amber or cream wash to make them read distinctly. The corridor Jesse stands in is lighter and more open.

Primary character: Man in his early 40s. Short buzz-cut hair. Neatly trimmed short beard and mustache. Brown eyes. V-neck long sleeve shirt in deep blue (#100299) watercolor wash. Charcoal pants. Relaxed, unposed. Standing at the doorway threshold, weight slightly forward, looking into the room. Expression: calm, clear-eyed, slightly arrested — the moment of recognition without drama.

Scene description: A formal meeting room behind glass or an open doorway, table and chairs visible with pre-placed decision cards on each seat. Room is empty of people. Jesse stands just outside the threshold, one hand possibly resting on the doorframe, looking in. The cards on the chairs are the focal detail that earns the first-second read. Background schematic linework in the corridor walls suggests ducts, pipes, or conduit — organizational systems running unseen behind everything.

Context box: Uppercase sans-serif text inside a solid white (#ffffff) rectangle with a sharp charcoal (#333333) border. Placed at the extreme bottom of the panel. Text: THE DECISION WAS MADE BEFORE THE MEETING

Tone guardrails: This image should feel like two people talking through a real problem in plain language. Not a motivational poster. Not corporate (no handshakes, whiteboards, or team huddles). Not inspirational abstraction. Grounded, human, and specific. The mechanical background supports the scene — it does not compete with it.

## Concept 2 — The Room

**Scene dynamic:** Jesse stands in a corridor or informal space in close conversation with a colleague — a brief, direct one-on-one exchange that is clearly happening before something larger. The colleague faces Jesse, engaged but not adversarial. In the background, soft and out of detail, is the suggestion of a conference room — the formal space they're both about to enter. The real meeting is happening here.

**First-second read:** Two people in close, quiet conversation just outside a larger space. The body language is engaged and direct. The viewer reads: this is the conversation that matters, not the one that comes next.

**Compositional approach:** Jesse and a colleague at roughly equal scale, center-left, facing each other at close range. The corridor or informal space is their primary environment. A soft-focus or schematic suggestion of a conference room appears in the right background — smaller, secondary. Equal scale between the two people communicates a peer dialogue, not a hierarchy.

## Concept 3 — The Reframe

**Unexpected angle:** Scale inversion. A large telephone handset dominates the center-right of the frame. In the lower-left corner, sitting on a surface, is a miniaturized version of a full conference room setup — a tiny table, tiny chairs, a tiny projection screen. The proportion is inverted: the informal tool (the phone call) is large; the formal event (the meeting) is small. Jesse stands at the edge of the frame, his hand resting lightly on the base of the large phone, looking at the tiny meeting setup with a calm, knowing expression.

**First-second read:** A large telephone next to a tiny meeting room. The phone is the important thing. The meeting is small.

**Why it works:** The viewer's expectation — that the formal meeting is the important thing — is reversed in a single frame. The phone call is depicted at the scale of significance it actually deserves. The meeting looks like a toy.
