I was promoted above someone who had been above me in the org chart for years.

I thought the transition was going fine. The working relationship felt solid. What I did not notice was what the history had done to my feedback.

For months, something in that history was shaping what I said and what I held back. When I went too soft, I told myself it was patience. When I went too hard, I told myself it was standards.

Neither was actually about their performance.

The org chart changes. The history does not disappear. It ends up in your feedback. In what you say and in what you do not say.

What does it take to give someone feedback that is actually about the work, when you both still carry the weight of what the org chart used to look like?
