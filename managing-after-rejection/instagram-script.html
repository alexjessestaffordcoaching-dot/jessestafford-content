I have managed people who were once above me in the org chart. People who trained me. People who had been at the company since before I was born. One who had originally interviewed me and decided not to hire me.

[pause]

In every single one of those cases, the history found its way into my feedback before I realized it was there.

When I went too soft, I told myself it was patience. When I went too hard, I told myself it was high standards.

[pause]

Neither was about their performance. Both felt exactly like real management.

What took me the longest to catch: the distortion looks completely normal from the outside. You cannot see it in any single conversation. You have to look at the pattern.

[pause]

Before your next feedback conversation with someone you have complicated history with, ask yourself if you would give that same feedback, the same way, to someone with no history between you at all.

That question tells you everything.
