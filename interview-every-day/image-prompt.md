## Shared Elements

**Core situation:** A person inside the organization who is technically "internal" but functionally unknown to the people who will decide their next role — because they never built connections beyond their own team.

**Context box text:** BEING INTERNAL IS NOT THE SAME AS BEING KNOWN

**Emotional tone:** Direct-and-focused — calm recognition, not distress. Jesse has just read the situation correctly. His expression is quiet and clear-eyed, the look of someone who has understood something they couldn't see before.

## Concept 1 — The System

**Visual metaphor:** A large circuit junction hub where many cables converge from across the organization. Jesse stands in the lower-left of the frame, smaller than the hub, his single cable trailing off behind him to the left into open white space — no node on the other end. He is near the hub, not at it. Looking toward it.

**First-second read:** A man standing slightly apart from a large, active, interconnected system. The hub is lit and full of cables. His side of the frame is mostly empty.

**Compositional approach:** Jesse occupies the lower-left quarter of the frame, smaller in scale than the hub. The hub fills the right two-thirds — large, active, many cables converging from the upper and right edges. The empty space between Jesse and the hub is intentional. His cable trails behind him to the left.

### Full Expanded Prompt

Generate a single-panel illustration in the Schematic Wash style for Jesse Stafford Coaching LLC. Topic: being present in an organization without being connected to the people who matter.

**COMPOSITION — most important:** Wide shot. The circuit junction hub occupies the right two-thirds of the frame — large, detailed, rendered in charcoal patent-drawing linework with many cables converging on it from the upper-right and right edge. The hub is active and interconnected. Jesse stands in the lower-left corner of the frame, noticeably smaller than the hub. The space between Jesse and the hub is mostly empty — negative space, white paper texture. A single cable or wire extends from Jesse's position off to the left behind him, trailing into open white space with no node attached. Jesse is looking toward the hub. He is not touching it, not operating it. He is near it — and his connection goes the wrong direction.

**Art style:** Crisp, uniform charcoal ink outlines (#333333), medium line weight throughout. Comic book precision — not sterile flat-art, not heavy superhero cross-hatching. Lines should suggest a technical patent drawing or a well-crafted indie comic panel. Loose, organic watercolor fills — visibly hand-painted. Color intentionally bleeds slightly outside the ink lines or leaves small white gaps at boundaries. Wet-edge textures and transparency variations are required — these are features, not errors. No digital gradients. No glow effects. No perfectly aligned fills. Background: clean white paper texture (#ffffff) with steampunk mechanical elements — gears, intricate dials, levers, grid-based schematics — rendered in charcoal linework as abstract environmental texture in the far background only. Background elements must not compete with the hub or the figure. 16:9 aspect ratio, single panel.

**Color palette:** Primary watercolor wash: #100299 (deep blue). Canvas and negative space: #ffffff. Linework and shadows: #333333. Shadows are a semi-transparent charcoal watercolor wash — never solid black fill. The hub can carry a warm amber or copper watercolor wash to read as active and lit. Jesse's shirt stays deep blue (#100299). Supporting colors must not compete with the blue/white/charcoal triad.

**Primary character:** Man in his early 40s. Short buzz-cut hair. Neatly trimmed short beard and mustache. Brown eyes. V-neck long sleeve shirt in deep blue (#100299) watercolor wash. Charcoal pants. Standing in the lower-left of the frame, turned slightly toward the hub on his right. Not touching any equipment. Expression: calm and clear-eyed — the quiet look of someone who has just understood something correctly. Not alarmed. Not defeated. Recognition.

**Scene:** The primary focal element is the circuit junction hub rendered in charcoal linework with patent-drawing precision — switches, terminals, connection points. Multiple cables converge on it from the upper and right portions of the frame. Behind Jesse, a single wire trails off to the left into open white space. He is physically near the hub. He simply was never wired into it. Background reinforces this with steampunk mechanical infrastructure — gears and dials in the far background that suggest the broader system. Negative space is intentional — do not fill the frame.

**Context box:** Uppercase sans-serif text inside a solid white (#ffffff) rectangle with a sharp charcoal (#333333) border. Placed at the bottom of the panel. Text: BEING INTERNAL IS NOT THE SAME AS BEING KNOWN

**Tone:** This image should feel like a moment of quiet realization. Not a motivational poster. Not corporate. Not dramatic. The hub is just a hub. The cable just goes the wrong way. The man just sees it. That is the whole image.

## Concept 2 — The Room

**Scene dynamic:** A small group of three or four professionals in an informal conversation — standing, engaged with each other, relaxed. Jesse stands nearby, same physical space, but slightly outside the natural orbit of the group. He is present. He is not part of the circle. No one is looking at him. No one is excluding him. They simply don't know him.

**First-second read:** A group of people in conversation — and one person just outside that conversation who everyone else hasn't noticed is there.

**Compositional approach:** The group of three or four occupies the center-right of the frame, leaning toward each other slightly, engaged. Jesse stands on the left side of the frame, same eye level, same environment, same casual posture — but the gap between him and the group is visible. He is looking toward them. They are not looking toward him.

## Concept 3 — The Reframe

**Unexpected angle:** Scale inversion — a name badge or ID card rendered large and prominent in the foreground, showing a name and photo. Behind it, small in the background, Jesse stands alone. The badge proves he belongs here. The empty space around him proves no one knows it yet.

**First-second read:** A large, official badge that says this person is authorized — and behind it, someone standing alone in a lot of empty space.

**Why it works:** The badge is the proof of "being internal." It does everything a badge is supposed to do. And it does nothing for what actually matters. The gap between the badge's authority and the person's isolation is the entire insight of the post — rendered in one image without a word of explanation.

He is not touching the badge — it floats in the foreground between him and the viewer, as if it were his credential on display. The space around Jesse is empty. No other people. No connected system. Just him, the credential, and a lot of space.
