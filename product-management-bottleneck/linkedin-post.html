The engineering leaders I know who are advancing fastest are not the ones writing the most code.

AI has made execution dramatically faster. The constraint in most software teams has shifted from how to build to what to build. Andrew Ng put numbers to what many technical leaders are quietly sensing: the traditional ratio of six engineers to one product manager is inverting at AI-native companies. The bottleneck is not building anymore.

What that means for your career is direct. If you built your value around execution depth, you are competing with tools that execute faster every month. The leaders who become hard to replace are the ones who know what should be built, for whom, and against what constraints. None of those skills compress with AI.

I learned this before AI had a name for it. The shift that accelerated my career had nothing to do with how fast I could build. It had everything to do with understanding the business beginning to end.

What are you doing right now that only you can do, not because you are fast, but because of what you know about your organization?
