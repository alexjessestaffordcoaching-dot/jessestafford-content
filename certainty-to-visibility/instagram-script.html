Your silence in meetings isn't silence. It's agreement.

The room can't see your doubt. Can't hear your thinking. They just see you not pushing back, and they read that as "she's fine with this."

So you get quiet. You wait for the moment when you're certain enough to speak.

That moment usually never comes.

And by then, the decision is locked in with your name on it.

Say something this week. Ask a question. Offer your partial perspective. Surface the concern. You don't need the answer to be in the conversation. You just need to show up.

You're great at the work. Let's make you impossible to ignore.
